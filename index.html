    How to calculate the distance between A and B in a 3 Dimensional coordinate system

    18/10/2018


    So how so you actually calculate this?

    Well let’s begin with actually showing what we are going to do.

    In this case we are going to calculate the distance between the Origin “(0;0;0)” and P “(5;5;5)”


    The picture below shows a 3 dimensional coordinate system.

    Every coordinate has 3 dimensional coordinates in them. The X coordinate, the y coordinate and the z coordinate.


    In this case the origin is “obviously” (0;0;0) and P is (5;5;5), first we need to calculate the distance between each coordinate and that is what the (x1-x2), (y1-y2) and (z1-z2) after that you calculate the distance between the two points in the “grid”.

    This you do with the equation below

    √( (x1-x2)^2 + (y1-y2)^2 + (z1-z2)^2 )


    And that is how you it, try it out and see the results.

    Your results for this specific example should be around: 8.66 coordinate units.
